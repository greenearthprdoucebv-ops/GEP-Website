## Abstract

This project charter defines the scope, goals, approach, and structure of the Green Earth Produce (GEP) project, carried out by a team of four students from Fontys University of Applied Science (PRJ4). The project aims to improve GEP's online visibility by delivering a professional website, and to modernise their internal business operations by improving their existing Odoo-based management system. This document serves as a reference for both the project team and GEP stakeholders throughout the 12-week project period (March 9 – May 30, 2026).

**Target readers:** Project team, GEP owner (Jenny Zhang), GEP intern, and Fontys course instructor.

## 1. Introduction / Management Summary

Green Earth Produce B.V. is a fresh fruit and vegetable trading company based at Fresh Park Venlo, Netherlands. GEP specialises in trade between Europe and China, with key products including ginger, garlic, Chinese cabbage, white radish, and pumpkin. Despite being an active business, GEP has a very limited online presence and relies on outdated tools to manage their day-to-day operations.

This project was initiated after a formal interview with GEP owner Jenny Zhang on March 3, 2026, during which GEP agreed to work with our Fontys PRJ4 team as their project provider. Two main challenges were identified:

- GEP lacks a proper, professional website — their current site is a basic placeholder with almost no useful content.
- Their internal system (Odoo-based) needs improvement to better support their business operations.

This project will run for 12 weeks. Jenny Zhang will oversee progress (30 minutes per week online) and a GEP intern will act as the main point of contact and representative of the company.

---

## 2. Business Case

GEP operates in a competitive market at Fresh Park Venlo, surrounded by established competitors such as Enes B.V., Hendrix & Seelen, and Eurofresh B.V. A competitor analysis conducted by the team confirmed that GEP's online presence is significantly weaker than most competitors, making it harder for potential customers to find and trust the company.

In addition, the lack of an effective internal management system creates operational inefficiencies that slow down GEP's day-to-day work. Addressing both of these issues will help GEP:

- Attract new customers and improve brand visibility online.
- Streamline internal operations and reduce manual work.
- Compete more effectively with other Fresh Park Venlo businesses.
- Create a more professional image for both local and international trade.

## 3. Approach

The project will be managed using a **Kanban task board on GitHub Projects**. All tasks will be created as GitHub Issues and moved across the following columns:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| **Backlog** | All tasks and ideas not yet started |
| **To Do** | Tasks planned for the current week |
| **In Progress** | Tasks actively being worked on |
| **In Review** | Tasks completed and awaiting peer review |
| **Done** | Tasks reviewed, approved, and closed |

Each task (issue) will include a title, description, assignee, and label (e.g. website, internal system, research, documentation). The board will be reviewed at the start of each week and updated throughout. Jenny Zhang and the GEP intern will be given read access to the board so they can follow progress at any time.

The team will hold short weekly stand-ups to sync on progress, and Jenny Zhang will be available for a 30-minute online feedback session each week.

### 3.1 In Scope

- Research and analysis of GEP's current website, online presence, and internal system.
- Design and development of a new, professional website for GEP.
- Improvement of GEP's existing Odoo-based internal management system.
- Regular feedback loops with Jenny Zhang and the GEP intern.
- Delivery of project documentation including this charter, progress updates, and a final report.

---

### 3.2 Out of Scope

- Full replacement of the Odoo system with a different platform.
- Ongoing maintenance or support after the project ends (May 30, 2026).
- Marketing campaigns or paid advertising for GEP.
- Hardware procurement or physical infrastructure changes.
- Legal or financial consulting for GEP.

## 5. Quality Management

**Quality Objectives:**
- All deliverables must meet the requirements confirmed with GEP.
- Work must be clear, functional, and usable by GEP without technical knowledge.

**Quality Assurance:**
- All tasks must pass peer review (moved to "In Review" on the GitHub Kanban board) before being marked as Done.
- Weekly feedback from Jenny Zhang is used to validate direction and quality.
- The GitHub board gives full visibility to all team members and GEP at any time.

**Quality Control:**
- Testing of the website and internal system will be conducted in the testing milestone (M7).
- Feedback from the GEP intern is collected continuously throughout the project.

## 6. Prerequisites

**Software Requirements:**
- Access to GEP's existing Odoo environment and current website (Open2Bizz).
- GitHub account and access to the project repository and Kanban board.
- Development tools: code editors, browser testing tools, design tools (e.g. Figma).
- Communication tools: Microsoft Teams, WhatsApp.

**Access Requirements:**
- Login credentials or admin access to GEP's current website and Odoo system.
- Access to any existing IT contracts or service agreements GEP has with external providers.

**People Requirements:**
- Jenny Zhang available for 30 minutes per week online for feedback and sign-off.
- GEP intern available to communicate requirements and relay Jenny's input to the team.
