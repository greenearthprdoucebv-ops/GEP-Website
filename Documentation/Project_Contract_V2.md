# Team Contract – Green Earth Produce Project

**University:** Fontys University of Applied Science
**Course:** PRJ4
**Date:** March 9, 2026

---

## Purpose

This contract sets the expectations, responsibilities, and standards of behaviour for all members of the PRJ4 project team. By signing this document, each member agrees to uphold the rules outlined below for the full duration of the project. The goal is to ensure a professional, fair, and productive working environment for everyone.

---

## 1. Attendance

- All team members are expected to attend every scheduled project hour without exception.
- If a member cannot attend, they must inform the **entire team at least 24 hours in advance** via the agreed communication channels.
- Last-minute absences (under 24 hours notice) are only acceptable in genuine emergencies (e.g. illness, family emergency). Evidence may be requested by the team.
- Repeated unexcused absences will be escalated to the university-provided supervisor.
- Missing a session does not remove responsibility — the absent member is still expected to complete their assigned work for that week.

---

## 2. Weekly Work Commitment

- Each team member is expected to deliver an **agreed amount of work every week**, as decided during the weekly planning session.
- Work must be submitted or updated on the GitHub Kanban board **before the end of each week**.
- If a member is struggling to complete their work, they must communicate this to the team **before the deadline**, not after.
- Work that is not submitted without prior communication will be treated as a breach of this contract.

---

## 3. Quality Standards

- All work must be completed to a standard that the team is collectively comfortable submitting or presenting.
- Work must be **peer-reviewed** by at least one other team member before being marked as done.
- Submitting work that is clearly incomplete or below the agreed standard without flagging it first is considered unprofessional behaviour.
- All documents uploaded to the **GitHub repository must be in Markdown (.md) format**.
- All documents delivered or presented to the **client (GEP) must be in PDF format**.

---

## 4. Communication

- The team will communicate through the following channels:
  - **WhatsApp** – for quick updates, informal communication, and urgent messages.
  - **Microsoft Teams** – for meetings, file sharing, and project discussions.
  - **Email** – for formal communication, especially with the client (GEP) and supervisor.
- All team members are expected to **respond to messages within 24 hours** on working days.
- Important decisions must be communicated to the full team and not made by one person alone.
- All client-facing communication (emails, messages to Jenny Zhang or the GEP intern) must be agreed upon by the team before being sent.

---

## 5. Meetings

- The team will hold a **weekly stand-up** to sync on progress, plan the week, and raise any blockers.
- Meetings will be held via Teams or on-site, as agreed per session.
- An agenda will be shared before each meeting where possible.
- A short summary of decisions made will be noted after each meeting and shared in the Teams channel.

---

## 6. Respect and Professionalism

- All team members must treat each other with respect at all times — during meetings, in written communication, and in person.
- Disagreements must be handled constructively. Personal attacks, dismissive behaviour, or passive disengagement are not acceptable.
- Credit for work must be given fairly and honestly. Taking credit for someone else's work is a serious breach of this contract.
- All team members have an equal voice regardless of role or experience.
- Unprofessional behaviour toward the client (GEP) or the university supervisor will be treated as a serious violation.

---

## 7. Conflict Resolution

- If a disagreement arises within the team, members are first expected to resolve it through open and respectful discussion.
- If no resolution is reached, the matter will be brought to the **university-provided supervisor** for mediation.
- The supervisor's decision is final and must be respected by all team members.

---

## 8. Fines & Consequences

To keep the team accountable in a fair and lighthearted way, the following system applies:

### Standard Fine – A Round of Coffee ☕

A round of coffee (for the full team) is owed by any member who breaks one of the following rules:

| Violation | Fine |
|---|---|
| Missing a session without 24h notice | 1 round of coffee |
| Failing to submit agreed work without prior communication | 1 round of coffee |
| Not responding to team messages within 24h (working days) | 1 round of coffee |
| Being more than 15 minutes late to a meeting without notice | 1 round of coffee |
| Sending client-facing communication without team approval | 1 round of coffee |

### Additional Fine – Supervisor Decision ☕+

For more serious or repeated unprofessional behaviour (e.g. disrespect, repeated violations, dishonesty), an additional fine or consequence may be imposed. This requires:

1. The behaviour to be clearly documented by the team.
2. The matter to be brought to the **university-provided supervisor**.
3. The supervisor to review the situation and decide on an appropriate consequence.

The team may not impose additional fines without supervisor approval.

---

## 9. Amendments

- This contract may be updated if all team members and the supervisor agree to a change.
- Any amendment must be documented, dated, and signed by all members.
