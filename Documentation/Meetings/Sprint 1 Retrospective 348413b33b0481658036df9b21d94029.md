# Sprint 1 Retrospective

Date: April 20, 2026
Sprint: Sprint 1
Status: ✅ Done
Type: 🪞 Retrospective

Sprint 1 focused on establishing the project foundation — understanding the client's needs, creating Figma designs for each page, setting up the technical infrastructure, and adopting agile practices. Each team member was assigned one page to design, ensuring equal workload distribution. Below is a per-person reflection on what worked, what didn't, and what we want to improve going into Sprint 2.

---

## Preslav (Scrum Master)

### What Went Well

- Took on the Scrum Master role and kept things organized — all meetings (client, coach, internal) were documented and Sprint Planning ran smoothly.
- The team completed all Figma designs on time. The one-page-per-person split worked well for keeping things balanced and giving us a clear visual direction.
- We came out of the client meetings with a strong understanding of GEP's core goal: expanding from the German market into the Netherlands.
- When a teammate couldn't make the pitch, the rest of the team covered it without any issues.

### What Didn't Go Well

- Early Navbar and Footer work had to be thrown out after the moodboard direction changed. Lesson learned: lock in the high-level design before starting on individual components.
- Mr. Salz pointed out that our GitHub commit messages weren't professional enough. A naming convention needs to be introduced.
- Absence communication was lacking — key roles like Pitch Lead were almost left uncovered because there was no handoff plan.

### What Can We Improve

- Finalize the overall design direction (moodboard, palette) before anyone starts building components.
- Introduce a commit convention (`feat:`, `fix:`, `docs:`) and enforce it team-wide from Sprint 2.
- Set up a simple process for reassigning responsibilities when someone can't attend.

### Technical Work

- Designed the system architecture (CBMD-based) and created a diagram to align the team on the tech stack.
- Set up the Supabase database (PostgreSQL) and organized the shared workspace.
- Configured Vercel hosting and deployed a test page to verify the CI/CD pipeline.
- Evaluated different hosting and database options to find the most efficient setup.

- Created a moodboard and the initial homepage design in Figma, which gave us a clear visual starting point.

- Continue doing peer reviews — they made a real difference in getting the team on the same page.

## Lukas

### What Went Well

- Joined the team visit to the GEP office and warehouse — seeing the business in person helped understand the real-world context behind the project.
- Prepared and asked questions during the client meeting, which gave us clear, usable requirements to work with.
- Owned the contact page end-to-end — designed it in Figma and then built an HTML/CSS prototype to test the layout in the browser.
- Started researching WCAG 2.2 guidelines early to keep accessibility in mind from the design phase.
- Merged the contact page design into the shared Figma sandbox to maintain consistency across pages.
- Improved PDP documentation by expanding descriptions and adding evidence of learning (e.g., documenting how YouTube tutorials were used to learn Figma).

### What Didn't Go Well

- Getting started with Figma was slow — had to rely on tutorial videos to get up to speed, which ate into design time.
- Built a class diagram that turned out to be unnecessary — an ER diagram would have been the right choice for modeling the contact form data.
- Initial PDP entries were too brief and didn't show enough investigative effort.

### What Can We Improve

- Before creating a technical diagram, confirm with the team or coach that the diagram type is actually appropriate — saves wasted effort.
- Keep PDP entries detailed and up to date from day one, rather than backfilling later.
- When someone figures out a new tool, share the best learning resources with the rest of the team.

## Delly

### What Went Well

- Revised the MoSCoW prioritization based on earlier feedback and created the project plan — this gave the team a much stronger foundation.
- Cleaned up the project folder structure, making everything more organized and easier to find.
- Designed the About Us page in Figma and continued refining its layout across multiple weeks.
- Started engaging more actively with agile practices (sprint planning, retrospectives), which brought more clarity to how the team works together.
- Built and uploaded a website prototype to a separate branch so the team could track and review progress.
- Documented the data structure by adding an [ERD.md](http://ERD.md) file — a simple and accessible approach.

### What Didn't Go Well

- The AI-generated About Us page couldn't be properly integrated with the rest of the project pages, so it had to be rebuilt from scratch manually. That cost extra time.
- PDP and portfolio updates weren't consistent — documentation slipped behind during the sprint.
- Made the call to skip the class diagram since it didn't add value at this stage, but that decision should have been communicated to the team earlier.

### What Can We Improve

- Stay disciplined with weekly documentation — PDP and portfolio updates need to be consistent, not catch-up work.
- When skipping an artifact, explain the reasoning to the team so everyone stays aligned on what's being delivered and why.
- Focus on producing meaningful evidence rather than creating artifacts just to have them.
