|**Company** |Large supermarket chain (e.g. Rewe Germany)|
|**Location**|Germany                                    |

|**Language**|German, English                            |

**About:**
Marcus works for a large supermarket chain and is responsible for sourcing fresh produce at competitive prices. He manages multiple suppliers and needs to make quick, informed decisions. He is looking for reliable suppliers who can deliver consistent quality at scale.

**Goals:**

- Find a trustworthy supplier for ginger, garlic, and other Asian vegetables.
- Compare products, pricing, and availability quickly.
- Get in touch with GEP easily to start a business relationship.
- Understand GEP’s capacity and reliability before committing.

**Frustrations:**

- Suppliers with no online presence are hard to trust.
- Wasting time on back-and-forth emails just to get basic product info.
- No clear way to know if a supplier can meet large volume orders.

**What he needs from the website:**

- A clear product catalogue with availability info.
- A professional and trustworthy look and feel.
- Easy contact options (email, phone, inquiry form).
- Company background and certifications if available.
- Content in German or English.

|**Role**    |Owner                    |
|**Company** |Independent grocery store|
|**Location**|Netherlands              |
|**Computer skills**|Computer-literate|
|**Language**|Dutch, Arabic            |

**About:**
Fatima runs a small to mid-sized grocery store in the Netherlands that serves a diverse community. She buys fresh produce weekly and values personal relationships with her suppliers. She often looks for specific ethnic vegetables that are hard to find elsewhere, especially ginger and Chinese cabbage.

**Goals:**

- Find a local supplier for fresh Asian vegetables.
- Place orders quickly without too much hassle.
- Build a long-term relationship with a reliable supplier.
- Get good prices for regular, repeat orders.

**Frustrations:**

- Large wholesalers don’t always cater to smaller shops.
- Hard to find specific products like white radish or pumpkin in good quality.
- Prefers suppliers she can call or visit directly.

**What she needs from the website:**

- A clear list of products GEP sells.
- Contact info that is easy to find (phone, WhatsApp, location).
- A sense of who GEP is and that they are approachable.
- Dutch language support.

**About:**
Sophie works for a growing supermarket chain looking to expand its range of Asian and exotic vegetables. She is actively researching new suppliers online and evaluates them based on their website, product range, and professionalism. GEP is not on her radar yet — but this is exactly the type of customer GEP wants to reach.

**Goals:**

- Discover new suppliers for exotic fresh produce.
- Evaluate GEP’s credibility and product range online before making contact.
- Understand minimum order quantities and delivery options.
- Find a supplier who can grow with her company’s needs.

**Frustrations:**

- Suppliers with no website or a very poor website are immediately dismissed.
- Hard to find detailed product information without having to call first.
- Language barriers when dealing with non-European suppliers.

**What she needs from the website:**

- A professional, modern website that builds trust immediately.
- A clear and detailed product catalogue.
- Information about delivery, coverage area, and order minimums.
- Contact options in English or French.
