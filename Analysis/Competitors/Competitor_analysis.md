## About the Market

All competitors listed below operate in or around **Fresh Park Venlo**, Europe’s largest fresh food distribution hub. Located at the intersection of the A67 and A73 motorways in the Netherlands, Fresh Park Venlo is home to over 100 companies dealing in fresh produce, logistics, and food wholesale. This makes it a highly competitive environment where GEP operates alongside many similar businesses.

## 1. Enes B.V.

**About:**
Enes B.V. was founded in 2004 in Grubbenvorst (near Venlo) and has grown from a 300m² facility to over 9,000m² on Fresh Park Venlo. They are a fresh fruit and vegetable wholesaler (Cash & Carry) with a strong focus on Turkish and German markets. Their subsidiary **Ceyda** handles import/export of produce from Spain, Italy, France, Turkey, Greece, Germany, and Chile.

**Products & Services:**

- Fresh fruit and vegetables (wholesale and Cash & Carry)
- Import and export of seasonal produce
- Cold chain transport and delivery
- Supply to gastronomy, wholesale, restaurants, grocery stores, and grocery chains across Europe

**Strengths:**

- Large and well-established operation (20+ years)
- Strong network in Turkey and Germany
- Diverse sourcing from multiple countries
- 24-hour loading facilities available
- iPad-based invoicing for Cash & Carry customers (tech-forward)
- Cold chain vehicles with refrigeration and antibacterial features

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for GEP:**

- Limited online presence found
- Primarily focused on Turkish and German customer base — GEP may serve different markets
- Large size may mean less personal service for smaller clients

**About:**
FT Logistics B.V. is a logistics and transport company with over 30 years of experience handling perishable goods. They operate from Fresh Park Venlo and are focused on the cold chain — ensuring fresh produce is transported quickly and handled correctly from origin to destination. They also operate **FT Customs B.V.**, a customs clearance subsidiary at the same address.

**Products & Services:**

- Temperature-controlled road transport
- Warehousing and distribution of fresh/perishable goods
- Customs brokerage and formalities (via FT Customs B.V.)
- Forwarding and international logistics
- Active in Rotterdam Port area

**Strengths:**

- 30+ years of experience with perishable goods logistics
- Dual operation (logistics + customs) under one roof — convenient for clients
- Strategic location at Fresh Park Venlo with Rotterdam Port connections
- Mid-size company (51–100 employees) with solid operational capacity
- Focus on speed and precision for fresh goods handling

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for GEP:**

- Primarily a **logistics/transport** company, not a fresh produce wholesaler — indirect competitor but relevant in the supply chain
- Limited online presence and marketing visibility found
- No social media presence identified

## 3. Hendrix & Seelen (Hendrix Fruit & Vegetables + P. Seelen Grubbenvorst B.V.)

|                   |                                                                       |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Location**       |Venrayseweg 182 (Hendrix) & Venrayseweg 122D (Seelen), Fresh Park Venlo|
|**Hendrix Website**|www.hendrixfruit.nl                                                    |
|**Seelen Website** |www.seelen.nl / www.pseelen.nl                                         |

**About:**
As of July 2024, **Hendrix Fruit & Vegetables B.V.** and **P. Seelen Grubbenvorst B.V.** have merged under one umbrella organization: **Hendrix & van Leeuwen B.V.**, led by director Jeroen Hendrix. Both companies continue to operate independently but are now connected economically, operationally, and legally. This makes them a notably stronger combined competitor.

**Hendrix Fruit & Vegetables** (founded 2010) was built around direct export to German wholesalers, food service, and processors — cutting out intermediaries to keep prices competitive. They source from Turkey, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, and beyond.

**P. Seelen Grubbenvorst B.V.** is a family business and international trading company with a trusted grower network in the Netherlands, Turkey, Spain, and Italy. They supply wholesale markets, retailers, supermarkets, and fresh produce processing companies across Western Europe. They have their own exclusive brands including Nanja (leeks, asparagus) and Sweet Lions (cherry tomatoes).

**Products & Services:**

- Fresh fruit and vegetables (direct export, no middlemen)
- Own exclusive brands and private-label products
- Supply to German wholesale, food service, retail, and processing sectors
- Import from multiple countries (Turkey, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Morocco, etc.)

**Strengths:**

- Now a merged, stronger combined entity
- Long-standing reputation and trusted grower relationships
- Own exclusive product brands (adds market differentiation)
- Direct exporter model — competitive pricing
- Strong focus on quality certifications (GlobalGAP, GRASP, Sedex/SMETA)
- Well-established European distribution, especially Germany

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for GEP:**

- Merger is recent (2024) — internal changes may cause disruption
- Heavy focus on Germany; GEP may target different geographies
- Large operation means flexibility for smaller clients may be limited

**Weaknesses / Opportunities for GEP:**

- Different focus (Turkish/ethnic food) vs GEP’s fresh produce
- Limited online presence and basic digital footprint

## Key Takeaways for GEP

- **Online presence** is a clear gap across most competitors — improving GEP’s website and digital visibility is a real opportunity to stand out.
- **Hendrix & Seelen** is the strongest combined competitor after their 2024 merger. Worth monitoring closely.
- **Enes B.V.** is well-established and tech-forward operationally but has limited online visibility.
- **Bengü Food** is a different type of competitor (ethnic wholesale) with limited direct overlap.
- GEP has an opportunity to differentiate through a **better online presence and a modern internal system** — which is exactly what this project aims to deliver.
