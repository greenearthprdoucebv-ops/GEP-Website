# Domain Provider Research – Green Earth Produce Website

**Author:** PRJ4 Team – Fontys University, Venlo
**Date:** June 2026
**Project:** Green Earth Produce (GEP) – Website Delivery
**Status:** In Progress / Research Phase

---

## 1. Purpose

As part of delivering a professional website for Green Earth Produce B.V., the team needs to decide on a domain registrar. This document investigates the most relevant domain providers, compares their pricing and features, and gives a recommendation based on GEP's needs — namely a simple, low-cost, and professional domain for a small B2B trading company.

GEP currently uses Open2Bizz for their existing placeholder site, so we also need to check whether their domain is already registered there or if it needs to be transferred / newly registered.

---

## 2. What GEP Needs

Before comparing providers, it's useful to outline what actually matters here:

- A professional domain (ideally something like `greenearthproduce.nl` or `.com`)
- Low cost — GEP is a small company and this is a student project, budget matters
- Simple management — Jenny Zhang is not a technical person
- Reliable uptime and DNS management
- Ideally includes WHOIS privacy (so GEP's personal details aren't publicly exposed)
- SSL certificate support (for HTTPS — important for trust and SEO)

---

## 3. Domain Extension Options

The two most logical extensions for GEP are:

| Extension | Reasoning |
|---|---|
| `.nl` | GEP is based in the Netherlands (Fresh Park Venlo). Most local customers will expect this. Usually cheaper than .com for NL businesses. |
| `.com` | Better for international trade (GEP trades between Europe and China). More universally recognisable. |

**Recommendation:** Register both if budget allows, and redirect `.nl` → `.com` (or vice versa). If only one, `.nl` is more appropriate given GEP's local base, but `.com` is more future-proof for international credibility.

The following providers were researched based on popularity in the Netherlands, pricing transparency, and ease of use.

### 4.2 Transip (NL-based)

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| `.com` (Year 1) | ~€12–14 |
| `.nl` (Year 1) | ~€4–6 |
| Renewal | Reasonable, no hidden fees |
| WHOIS Privacy | Included |
| SSL | Free Let's Encrypt included |

---

### 4.3 Hostinger

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| `.com` (Year 1) | ~€8–10 (often on sale) |
| `.nl` (Year 1) | ~€6–8 |
| Renewal | Noticeably higher than year 1 price |
| WHOIS Privacy | Included |
| SSL | Free |
| Interface | Very beginner-friendly |
| HQ | Lithuania (EU) |
| Notes | Often has aggressive discounts for year 1. Renewal costs jump. Good if the team is also hosting with them. |

**Verdict:** Good for budget if paying year 1, but **watch the renewal price**. Not ideal as a standalone domain registrar.

---

### 4.4 GoDaddy

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| `.com` (Year 1) | ~€1–2 (promo) / ~€18+ renewal |
| `.nl` (Year 1) | ~€5 (promo) |
| Renewal | Significantly higher — this is their model |
| WHOIS Privacy | Paid add-on |
| SSL | Paid add-on |
| Interface | Cluttered with upsells |
| HQ | US |
| Notes | Famous for cheap year-1 pricing but the renewal model is not great. WHOIS privacy being a paid extra is a red flag for a small business. |

**Verdict:** Avoid. The first-year price is a trap — renewal costs are high and the constant upselling makes it annoying for non-technical users like Jenny.

---

### 4.5 Open2Bizz (current provider)

| Feature | Details |
|---|---|
| Domain pricing | Not publicly listed — bundled into website packages |
| Flexibility | Low — tied to their CMS/website builder |
| Control | Limited — you don't "own" the domain independently |
| Notes | GEP's existing site is on Open2Bizz. If the domain is already registered there, transferring it is possible but adds complexity. |

**Verdict:** Not recommended as a dedicated domain registrar. If GEP's domain is currently registered here, we should investigate transferring it.

## 6. Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Transip**

For GEP specifically, Transip is the most sensible choice. It's a Dutch company, offers Dutch-language support, produces invoices in a format that works for local businesses, and has competitive `.nl` pricing. It also has a solid reputation in the Netherlands — Jenny Zhang or the GEP intern can manage it without help from the team after handover.

**Budget recommendation: Namecheap**

If cost is the only priority, Namecheap wins. It's transparent, cheap, and reliable. The only downside is it's US-based, which may be slightly less convenient for GEP when dealing with renewals or support.

**Avoid: GoDaddy** — the renewal pricing model and paid WHOIS privacy make it a poor long-term choice for a small business.
