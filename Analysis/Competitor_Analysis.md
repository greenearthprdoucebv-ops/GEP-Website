## 6. Design Recommendations for GEP

### 1. Invest in a hero product image or video

TFC's cinematic fruit flat-lay is the most powerful first impression in the sector. A commissioned hero photograph of GEP's actual produce â€” or a short video reel â€” would place GEP above KX Food and OGL immediately. A blurred gradient (OGL) or world map (Vision Fresh) does not show the product.

### 2. Avoid the tired all-green palette

Three of four competitors already use green as their dominant colour. GEP should use green only as a supporting tone, not the sole brand driver â€” otherwise the site is instantly forgettable alongside competitors.

### 3. Custom build â€” not Wix or an unmodified template

Vision Fresh's Wix constraints are visibly damaging its credibility. KX Food's 2016 WordPress template shows in every spacing decision. GEP needs a proper custom-built or premium CMS site to achieve design quality competitive with TFC.

### 4. Make certifications a design feature, not a footer afterthought

All four competitors have certifications (IFS, GlobalGAP) but bury them. GEP should design a dedicated trust section on the homepage â€” certification badges with short explanations of what they mean for buyers.

### 5. Use whitespace and typography as design tools

None of the four sites use whitespace or type hierarchy as deliberate design choices. A clean, generous layout with a considered heading font will make GEP instantly look more premium than the template-default layouts seen across all competitors.
